## 1. Understanding the Shared Responsibility Model (approx. 45 min)

### Understanding the Concept

*   **Task:** Explain the concept of the Shared Responsibility Model in your own words. What exactly does it mean that cloud providers and customers share responsibility, and why is this so crucial for security and operations in the cloud?

### Examples of Responsibilities

*   **Task:** Create a clear table or list that outlines concrete examples of responsibilities in the areas of **Security**, **Compliance**, and **Operations**. Clearly indicate which party (Cloud Provider vs. Customer) is responsible for which specific aspect. Think of at least three examples per area.

### Self-Reflection

*   Where do you see the greatest potential misunderstandings or challenges in applying the Shared Responsibility Model in practice? How could these best be addressed?

## 2. Exploring IaaS (Infrastructure as a Service) (approx. 30 min)

### Definition and Core Characteristics

*   **Task:** Define Infrastructure as a Service (IaaS) and describe its essential characteristics. Name at least three distinctive properties that differentiate IaaS from other cloud service models.

### Common Use Cases

*   **Task:** Identify and describe three common use cases for IaaS. Briefly explain for each case why IaaS is a particularly suitable and advantageous solution in that specific scenario.

### Self-Reflection

*   If you had a small startup that wanted to develop a new web application, when would you choose IaaS as your infrastructure base and when would you rather not?

## 3. Understanding PaaS (Platform as a Service) (approx. 30 min)

### Definition and Core Characteristics

*   **Task:** Define Platform as a Service (PaaS) and outline its core characteristics. What kind of abstraction does PaaS offer compared to IaaS, and which components does the provider manage?

### Advantages and Limitations

*   **Task:** Discuss the main advantages and potential limitations of using PaaS. Consider at least two specific advantages and two possible disadvantages or challenges.

### Self-Reflection

*   In which types of projects or for which development teams could PaaS best leverage its strengths, and where might it reach its limits or be less suitable?

## 4. Analyzing SaaS (Software as a Service) (approx. 30 min)

### Definition and Core Characteristics

*   **Task:** Define Software as a Service (SaaS) and describe its central characteristics. What fundamentally distinguishes SaaS from traditional on-premise software, and how does this affect the user?

### Everyday Examples

*   **Task:** Name and describe five common everyday examples of SaaS applications that you may have used yourself or heard of. Briefly explain why they are considered SaaS.

### Self-Reflection

*   Consider how SaaS has changed our daily lives and the way businesses operate in recent years. What is the biggest impact you personally perceive from SaaS?
