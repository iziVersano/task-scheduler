## Spot Instances
- Leverage unused AWS capacity at significant discounts (up to 90% in some cases, but often less in practice).
- Ideal for interruptible and non-critical workloads such as:
  - **Batch jobs**: Workloads scheduled during off-peak hours (e.g., weekends or nights) to capitalize on low spot prices.
  - **ETL (Extract, Transform, Load)**: Efficient for processing large datasets incrementally, e.g., converting 1M records on weekends.
  - **Render farms**: Distributing rendering tasks across multiple GPU-enabled EC2 instances.
  - **Stateless containers**: Well-suited for the ephemeral characteristics of spot instances.
- Spot instances can be reclaimed by AWS with a two-minute warning.
- Pricing varies based on market demand and instance types.
- Spot Fleets enable specifying required capacity (e.g., 32 instances for six hours), with automated bidding.

> 🏢 Real World: Movie studios use spot instances on AWS to run large-scale rendering jobs for CGI-animated films, saving significant costs during production.

## Reserved Instances (RIs)
- Commit to using a specific instance type for 1 or 3 years, unlocking significant discounts (up to 72% for 3 years, all upfront).
- Reserved Instances do not depend on actual usage—costs occur regardless of whether the instance is active or idle.
- Variants:
  - **Standard Reserved Instances**: Fixed instance type; highest discount, least flexibility.
  - **Convertible Reserved Instances**: Allow some flexibility in changing instance families; lower discount than standard RIs.
- Best suited for predictable, consistent workloads that remain active over long periods (e.g., 24/7 workloads).

| **Plan**          | **Discount**   | **Flexibility**                | **Term Lengths**         |
|--------------------|----------------|--------------------------------|--------------------------|
| Standard RI        | Up to 72%      | Fixed instance type            | 1 or 3 years            |
| Convertible RI     | Lower discount | Flexible with instance families | 1 or 3 years            |

> 🏢 Real World: E-commerce platforms with consistent year-round traffic can save costs by running their ecommerce website on Reserved Instances.

## Savings Plans
- Commitment-based pricing that is application-agnostic, allowing allocation across various AWS services.
  - E.g., EC2, Fargate, and Lambda compute usage.
- Commit to a specific amount of usage (e.g., $1/hour) for a fixed term of 1 or 3 years.
- Discounts similar to Reserved Instances.
- Two types:
  - **Compute Savings Plan**: Greatest flexibility; applicable across AWS compute options.
  - **EC2 Instance Savings Plan**: Lower flexibility; largest savings (region or instance-family specific).

| **Plan**                    | **Scope**            | **Discount** | **Comment**                                |
|-----------------------------|-------------------|-------------|---------------------------------------|
| Compute Savings Plan        | Global           | Lower       | Works for multiple services (EC2, Lambda, Fargate). |
| EC2 Instance Savings Plan   | Regional/Specific | Higher      | Specific instance family and region restrictions. |

> 🏢 Real World: A global SaaS company can commit to a savings plan of $10K/month for compute infrastructure usage across different services, reducing costs for their dynamically changing workloads.

## On-Demand Instances
- Pay for compute instances by the second (or hour) without any long-term commitments.
- Highest flexibility, suitable for unpredictable workloads or experimentation.
- Best for:
  - Short-term workloads, including POCs, testing, and development.
  - Variable demand usage patterns (e.g., seasonal spikes or sudden demand surges).

| **Use Case**             | **Recommendation**                 |
|--------------------------|------------------------------------|
| Short-term workloads     | On-demand instances               |
| Known, steady workloads  | Reserved Instances/Savings Plans  |

> 🏢 Real World: A sports website could use on-demand instances to handle surges during championship matches and scale back after the event ends.

## Stateless Containers
- Containers are inherently stateless: All data inside the container is temporary and is destroyed when the container terminates.
- Persistent storage (e.g., EBS, S3) must be explicitly configured if needed.
- Spot instances are highly suited for stateless container workloads due to their ephemeral nature.

> 🏢 Real World: A media company operating containerized Kubernetes clusters could use spot instances for scaling short-lived, stateless microservices dynamically during peak hours.

## Use Cases for Pricing Models
- **Spot Instances**: Temporary, cost-sensitive, flexible workloads (e.g., batch jobs, rendering, ephemeral apps).
- **Reserved Instances**: Predictable, consistent, 24/7-like workloads.
- **Savings Plans**: Broad commitment-based pricing for varied workloads, great for diverse service consumption.
- **On-Demand Instances**: Suitable for new projects, testing, transient workloads, or unpredictable demand spikes.

### Comparative Analysis of Pricing Models
| **Model**      | **Cost**       | **Flexibility** | **Commitment** | **Best For**                                                 |
|----------------|----------------|-----------------|----------------|-------------------------------------------------------------|
| Spot Instances | Lowest          | High            | None           | Batch, ETL, rendering, Kubernetes, flex workloads          |
| Reserved (RI)  | Lower (up to 72% discount) | Medium-Low      | 1 or 3 years    | Predictable 24/7 workloads.                                 |
| Savings Plans  | Comparable to RI | High            | 1 or 3 years    | Mixed workloads, includes Lambda and Fargate flexibility    |
| On-Demand      | Highest         | Highest         | None           | New apps, testing, dev, unanticipated demand spikes         |

> 🏢 Real World: A fintech startup might mix on-demand for testing, some RIs for base loads, and spot instances for backend processing to optimize costs while ensuring flexibility and savings.
